# Welcome to Arborito Library

Hello, traveler.

You have entered a living map of human knowledge. This is not just a library; it is a **decentralized knowledge source** where every node represents a concept, a skill, or a story waiting to be discovered.

@section: What is this place?

This is the **Official Arborito curriculum**. It is a community-curated collection of essential topics ranging from Computer Science to Humanities. It is designed to be:

*   **Open Source:** Maintained by the community.
*   **Free:** Accessible to everyone, forever.
*   **Living:** Constantly growing and evolving.

@section: How to navigate

1.  **Explore:** Drag the canvas to move around the **Study Branches**.
2.  **Expand:** Click on the circles to reveal sub-topics.
3.  **Learn:** Click on the leaf nodes (📄) to open lessons.
4.  **Track:** Your progress is saved locally in your browser.

@section: Images, audio, and video

Lessons may include **rich media**: for example images (and sometimes audio) from **Wikimedia Commons** and embedded video from **YouTube**, so the curriculum can stay illustrated and open without giving up widely usable materials. **Arborito** may ask for your consent before loading those third-party domains (privacy and your control). When Commons material is used, **licenses** and attribution are respected.

@section: Ready to start?

There is no specific order. Follow your curiosity. Find a **Branch** that interests you and start climbing.

@section: Collaborate

Arborito is **open source**: the curriculum lives in a repository where you can propose new lessons, fixes, or translations. Some branches are still empty or in draft—those are places where **community contributions** matter most.

> *"Knowledge is the only resource that grows when shared."*

*Happy learning!*
