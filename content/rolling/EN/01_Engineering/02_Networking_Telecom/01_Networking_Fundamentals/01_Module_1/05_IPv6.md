# IPv6: more than “more addresses”

**IPv6** removes address scarcity and simplifies concepts (no broadcast, broad **multicast**). But it introduces **NDP** instead of classic ARP, **SLAAC**, and new **filtering** habits. This lesson prepares routing and services without myths.

@section: Representation

Eight groups of 16 bits in **hex**, abbreviating `::` once. **Link-local** `fe80::/10` exists on active interfaces.

**Global unicast** under ISP-assigned prefixes or **ULA** `fc00::/7` for internal networks (similar to RFC1918 with nuances).

@section: Neighbors and NDP

**Neighbor Discovery** replaces ARP: **NS/NA** messages resolve MACs; **RS/RA** from routers advertise prefixes and flags (managed/other).

**SLAAC** auto-configures addresses; **DHCPv6** can supply DNS and options.

@section: Headers and extensions

A fixed base header plus **extension headers** (fragmentation, routing, etc.). Implications for **ACLs** and stateful firewalls inspecting layer 4.

@section: Dual-stack and transition

**Dual-stack** IPv4+IPv6 side by side is common. Mechanisms like **6to4**, **Teredo**, **DS-Lite** exist but evaluate real operational support.

**NAT64/DNS64** reaches IPv4-only content from IPv6-only networks.

@section: Security

“IPv6 is everywhere even if you don’t believe it”: link-local interfaces are active. **Filter** at the edge and on hosts; do not assume absence of traffic.

@section: Common mistakes

* Thinking IPv6 “is unused” and leaving policies wide open.
* Misconfigured **RAs** → hosts without DNS via DHCPv6 when expected.
* Ignoring **tunnel MTU** issues with PMTUD.

@section: Suggested lab

1. Enable IPv6 on a test LAN and observe **RAs** with a real router or `radvd`.
2. Use `ping -6` and `ip -6 neigh` to observe neighbors.
3. List `fe80::` interfaces and explain why they are link-local.
