# Verb *to be* and *there is/are*

***To be*** expresses **identity**, **state**, and **location**. ***There is/are*** introduces **existence** in a place (like “there is/there are” as an existential pattern, not “over there”).

@section: 1. Present forms

| Pronoun | Affirmative | Negative | Common contraction |
|---------|-------------|----------|--------------------|
| I | *am* | *am not* | *I’m not* |
| he/she/it | *is* | *is not* | *isn’t* |
| you/we/they | *are* | *are not* | *aren’t* |

Affirmative contractions: *I’m, you’re, he’s, she’s, it’s, we’re, they’re*.

@section: 2. A1 uses

**Identity / job**

* *I’m a student.*
* *She’s an engineer.*

**State**

* *I’m tired.*
* *We’re ready.*

**Location**

* *I’m at home.*
* *The keys are on the table.*

**Origin**

* *I’m from Spain.*

@section: 3. Questions

Inversion:

* *Are you ready?*
* *Is she your sister?*

Wh‑ questions:

* *Where are you from?*
* *How old are you?*

Short answers:

* *Yes, I am.* / *No, I’m not.*
* *Yes, it is.* / *No, it isn’t.*

@section: 4. *There is* / *there are*

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Singular | *There is a problem.* / *There’s a café.* |
| Plural | *There are two parks.* |
| Negative | *There isn’t a lift.* / *There aren’t any seats.* |
| Question | *Is there a bank near here?* |

**Concept:** *there* here is an **existential starter**, not “allí”.

Light touch: *any* in questions/negatives:

* *There aren’t any chairs.*

@section: 5. *To be* vs present simple (quick contrast)

* *I am tired.* (state now)
* *I work a lot.* (habit/general)

@section: 6. Model dialogues

**Hotel**

> **A:** **Is there** a restaurant in the hotel?  
> **B:** Yes, **there is** one on the second floor.  
> **A:** **Are there** vegetarian options?  
> **B:** Yes, **there are**.

**Intro**

> **A:** **Are you** the new intern?  
> **B:** Yes, **I am**. **I’m** from Barcelona.

@section: 7. Common mistakes

* *I am agree* → *I agree.*
* *There is two problems* → *There **are** two problems.*

@section: 8. Lab

1. Write eight sentences mixing identity, state, location, and *there is/are*.
2. Turn six affirmatives into questions with inversion.
3. Describe your neighborhood with five *there is/are* sentences.

@section: 9. Summary

You can use ***am/is/are*** and ***there is/are*** confidently at A1. Next: **home, family, possessives, demonstratives**.

@section: 10. Contractions in listening

Train *I’m, isn’t, aren’t* with short clips; turn subtitles off on repeat listens.

@section: 11. *It’s* vs *its*

* *It’s* = *it is/it has*; *its* = possessive (*The hotel has its own gym*).

@section: 12. *some/any* with *there are*

* *There are **some** chairs outside.* *There aren’t **any** tickets left.*
