# City, transport, and directions

Moving around a city in English requires a small set of **questions**, **place phrases**, and **imperatives** for routes. This chapter also covers **polite** ways to stop someone and **confirm** what you heard.

@section: 1. Essential questions

| Question | Use |
|----------|-----|
| *Where is the station?* | Location |
| *Where are the toilets?* | Plural facilities |
| *How can I get to the museum?* | Route/mode |
| *Is it far?* | Distance |
| *How long does it take?* | Duration |
| *Which bus goes to…?* | Line choice |

Sample answers:

* *It’s **next to** the bank.*
* *Go **straight ahead** and **turn left**.*
* *About **ten minutes on foot**.*

@section: 2. Place phrases

| Phrase | Idea |
|--------|------|
| *next to* | beside |
| *between A and B* | between |
| *in front of* | facing side |
| *behind* | rear |
| *opposite* | across from |
| *on the corner* | at the corner |
| *at the traffic lights* | at lights |

*Near/close to* vs *far from*.

@section: 3. Transport patterns

* *by bus / by train / by tram / by plane*
* *I **take** the bus to work.*
* *five minutes **on foot***

Tickets:

* *a single ticket*, *a return* (UK) / *round trip* (US)

@section: 4. Giving directions

1. *Go straight on until you see …*
2. *Turn right at the …*
3. *It’s on your left.*

If unsure:

* *I’m sorry, I don’t know.*

@section: 5. Politeness and safety

Start with *Excuse me*. Prefer *Could you tell me…?* when stopping strangers.

**Confirm**:

* *So I turn left at the cinema?*

@section: 6. Model dialogues

> **A:** Excuse me, **where is** the nearest metro station?  
> **B:** **It’s opposite** the library. **Go straight** and **turn right** at the café.  
> **A:** **Is it** far?  
> **B:** No, **two minutes on foot**.

> **A:** **Which bus** goes to the airport?  
> **B:** **The N47**. **It stops** here.

@section: 7. Signage vocabulary

* *map, sign, ticket machine, platform, entrance, exit, lift/elevator*

@section: 8. Common mistakes

* *How I can…?* → *How **can** I…?*
* *take bus* → *take **the** bus*
* *arrive to* → *arrive **at** the station* / *arrive **in** London*
