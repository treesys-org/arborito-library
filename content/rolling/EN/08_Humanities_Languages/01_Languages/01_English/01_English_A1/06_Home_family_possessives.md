# Home, family, and possessives

Talking about **where you live** and **who people are** is core A1. This chapter combines **housing vocabulary**, **family words**, **possessive determiners** (*my, your, his, her, our, their*), and **demonstratives** (*this/that/these/those*).

@section: 1. Rooms and home words

| Word | Gloss |
|------|-------|
| *kitchen* | cooking area |
| *living room* / *lounge* | main sitting area |
| *bedroom* | sleeping room |
| *bathroom* | bath/toilet room |
| *hall* / *hallway* | corridor |
| *balcony* | balcony |
| *garden* | garden |

Examples:

* *The kitchen is small but bright.*
* *We have two bedrooms.*

@section: 2. Furniture and objects

* *table, chair, sofa, bed, wardrobe, desk, lamp, fridge, microwave*

Prepositions (quick review):

* *on the wall*, *under the bed*, *in the drawer*

@section: 4. Possessive determiners

They stand **before** the noun—**no extra article**:

| OK | Not OK |
|----|--------|
| *my phone* | *the my phone* |
| *her brother* | *the her brother* |

Disambiguate “his/her” like English does—Spanish “su” ambiguity disappears.

@section: 5. Demonstratives

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|----------|--------|
| Near | *this* | *these* |
| Far | *that* | *those* |

* *This is my brother.*
* *These are our keys.*

@section: 6. *Have* vs *there is*

* *I have a garden* (possession)
* *There is a park behind the house* (existence in space)

@section: 7. Model dialogues

> **A:** **Is this** your new flat?  
> **B:** Yes! **This is** the living room and **that’s** the kitchen.  
> **A:** **Are those** your plants?  
> **B:** Yes, **they’re** mine.

> **A:** **Do you have** siblings?  
> **B:** Yes, **I have** one sister. **Her** name is Laura.

@section: 8. Common mistakes

* *the my house* → *my house*
* *My parents lives* → *My parents **live***

@section: 9. Lab

1. Label a drawing of a home with 12 English words.
2. Write ~120–150 words introducing your (real or fictional) family with possessives.
3. Record a 90‑second “home tour” using *this/that* and *we have*.

@section: 10. Summary

You can describe **home**, **family**, **possession**, and **pointing** with demonstratives. Next: **city, transport, directions**.

@section: 11. *‑room* compounds

*bedroom, bathroom, living room, dining room* — one sentence: *Our **living room** is sunny.*

@section: 12. Family tree sketch

Label *grandparents, parents, cousins*; say two true relationships aloud.

@section: 13. *Whose* (light intro)

* *Whose bag is this?* — *It’s **mine**.*

@section: 14. Agreement trap

* *This **are** my keys* → *These **are** my keys.*
