# Numbers, time, dates, and prices

Numbers show up everywhere at A1: age, hotel room, flight, menu prices, appointment times. This chapter connects **how to say them** with **which question to ask**.

@section: 1. Cardinals 0–20 and *-teen* vs *-ty*

| Number | Word |
|--------|------|
| 0 | *zero* / *oh* (phones) |
| 1–12 | *one … twelve* |
| 13–19 | *thirteen … nineteen* |
| 20,30,… | *twenty, thirty, … ninety* |

**Minimal pairs:** *thirteen/thirty*, *fifteen/fifty*, *eighteen/eighty*. Stress matters.

@section: 2. 21–99

* *twenty-one*, *thirty-two*, *ninety-nine*.

*One hundred*; *two hundred* (no *s* on *hundred* after a multiplier).

@section: 3. Phones and digits

Group digits; pause clearly. Practice:

* *My number is …*
* *Let me repeat that slowly.*

@section: 4. Prices: *How much…?*

| Question | Context |
|----------|---------|
| *How much is this?* | Singular item. |
| *How much are these?* | Plural items. |
| *How much does it cost?* | Very natural alternative. |

Answers:

* *It’s two pounds fifty.*
* *They’re three euros each.*

@section: 5. Telling the time

* *It’s three o’clock.*
* *It’s three fifteen.* / *It’s a quarter past three.*
* *It’s half past four.*
* *It’s ten to six.*

*Past* = after the hour; *to* = minutes **toward** the next hour.

With 12h clocks, add *am/pm* for clarity.

@section: 6. Dates (A1 level)

* *on Monday*, *in July*, *in 2026* (*in twenty twenty-six*).

Birthdays:

* *My birthday is on May fifth.* (US order common; UK may use *the fifth of May*—recognize both.)

@section: 7. Age: avoid L1 calques

* Wrong: *I have twenty years.*
* Right: *I’m twenty.* / *I’m twenty years old.*

Question: *How old are you?*

@section: 8. Model dialogues

**Bakery**

> **A:** How much is this loaf?  
> **B:** It’s one euro twenty.  
> **A:** And those croissants?  
> **B:** They’re two fifty for two.

**Meeting time**

> **A:** What time is the meeting?  
> **B:** At a quarter past ten.

@section: 9. Common mistakes

* Same pronunciation for *-teen* and *-ty*.
* *How much **is** these?* → *How much **are** these?*

@section: 10. Lab

1. Read your phone number aloud 10 times with two different speeds.
2. Draw a clock and say eight times using *past* and *to*.
3. Listen to English price announcements and write down five numbers you hear.

@section: 11. Summary

You can handle **core cardinals**, **prices**, **clock time**, and **basic dates**. Next: **present simple** for routines where time and frequency matter.

@section: 12. Light fractions and measures

Learn whole phrases: *half a kilo*, *a quarter of an hour*—do not translate word‑for‑word from your L1.

@section: 13. Ordinals you actually need

* *first, second, third* for floors/dates (watch UK vs US floor numbering; ask *ground floor* if unsure).

@section: 14. Listening trap

If you confuse *fifteen* and *fifty*, ask: *Could you write it down, please?*

@quiz: Which is a clear informal way to say 3:15?
@option: half past three
@correct: three fifteen / a quarter past three
@option: ten to three
