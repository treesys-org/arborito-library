# Present simple: statements, negatives, and questions

The **present simple** describes **habits**, **general truths**, and **routines**. The rule that hurts most learners is **third person ‑s** (*she works*). This chapter moves from form to **living sentences**.

@section: 1. Core uses

1. Routines: *I start work at nine.*
2. General facts: *Cats like sleep.*
3. Stable preferences: *I like chocolate.*

Contrast (later levels): *I am working now* vs *I work on Fridays*.

@section: 2. Affirmative: third person ‑s

| Subject | *work* |
|---------|--------|
| I / you / we / they | *work* |
| he / she / it | *works* |

Spelling rules:

* *watch → watches*
* *study → studies*
* *go → goes*

Specials at A1: *have → has*; *do* as a lexical verb (*do homework*).

@section: 3. Negatives: *don’t* / *doesn’t*

* *I don’t like coffee.*
* *She doesn’t work on Sundays.*

Never add *‑s* to the main verb after *doesn’t*:

* Wrong: *She doesn’t works here.*
* Right: *She doesn’t work here.*

@section: 4. Questions: *Do* / *Does*

Yes/No:

* *Do you speak English?*
* *Does he know her?*

Short answers:

* *Yes, I do.* / *No, he doesn’t.*

Wh‑ questions:

* *Where do you live?*
* *What time does the train leave?*

@section: 5. Frequency adverbs

Usually **before** the main verb:

* *I always brush my teeth.*

With *be*, often **after**:

* *She is always late.*

*Never* is already negative: *I never drink soda.*

@section: 6. Useful routine collocations

| Collocation | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| *wake up* | *I wake up at seven.* |
| *have breakfast* | *We have breakfast together.* |
| *go to work* | *He goes to work by bus.* |
| *finish* | *I finish at six pm.* |

@section: 7. Model dialogues

> **A:** What do you do in the morning?  
> **B:** I get up early and I **always** drink tea. Then I **take** the metro.  
> **A:** **Does** your husband work from home?  
> **B:** No, he **doesn’t**. He works in a hospital.

@section: 8. Common mistakes

* Missing **‑s** (*He work*).
* *Do you be…?* for *to be* questions → use *Are you…?*
* Bad adverb position (*I drink always coffee*).

@section: 9. Lab

1. Write 10 sentences about your real day; highlight third person and check *‑s*.
2. Turn five affirmatives into **negatives**, then into **questions**.
3. Record 60 seconds using *always/usually/sometimes/never* at least once each.

@section: 10. Summary

You have the full A1 map for present simple. Next: ***to be*** and *there is/are*.

@section: 11. Stative verbs (bridge note)

Some verbs rarely take continuous meaning at A1 (*know, like*): *I **know** his name*, not *I am knowing*.

@section: 12. Weekly grid drill

Fill a Mon–Sun table with one morning/afternoon/evening sentence each—then narrate without reading.

@section: 13. Tiny interview questions

* *What do you do?* *Where do you work?* *Do you like your job?* — answer in three lines each.
