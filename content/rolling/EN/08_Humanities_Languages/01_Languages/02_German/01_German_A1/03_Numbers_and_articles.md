# Numbers and definite articles (der, die, das)

German nouns have **grammatical gender**. Definite articles in **nominative singular** are ***der*** (masculine), ***die*** (feminine), ***das*** (neuter). The **plural** article is always ***die*** for all genders.

Learn **noun + article** together: *die Tür*, *das Fenster*, *der Tisch*.

@section: 1. Numbers 0–19 and tens

| Number | German |
|--------|--------|
| 0 | *null* |
| 1–12 | *eins … zwölf* |
| 13–19 | *dreizehn … neunzehn* |

Tens: *zwanzig, dreißig, vierzig, fünfzig, sechzig, siebzig, achtzig, neunzig*.

21: *einundzwanzig* (unit + *und* + ten). Same pattern to 99.

100: *einhundert* / *hundert*.

@section: 2. Using numbers in phrases

* *Ich möchte **zwei** Brötchen, bitte.*
* *Der Bus kommt um **zehn** Uhr **fünfzehn**.*

@section: 3. Prices

* *Das kostet **drei Euro**.*
* *Zusammen **fünf Euro fünfzig**, bitte.*

Often *Euro* without plural *‑s* in everyday usage (*zwei Euro*).

@section: 4. Article table (nominative)

| Gender | Singular example | Plural |
|--------|------------------|--------|
| masc. | *der Mann* | *die Männer* |
| fem. | *die Frau* | *die Frauen* |
| neut. | *das Kind* | *die Kinder* |

@section: 5. Gender hints (not laws)

* *‑ung* often feminine (*die Zeitung*).
* *‑chen / ‑lein* often neuter (*das Mädchen*).
* Many nouns must simply be memorized.

@section: 6. Questions with quantities

* *Wie viel kostet das?*
* *Wie viele Personen?*

@section: 7. Model dialogues

**Bakery**

> **A:** Zwei Brötchen, bitte.  
> **B:** Das macht **zwei Euro vierzig**.

**Hotel**

> **A:** In welchem Stock ist das Zimmer?  
> **B:** **Im dritten Stock**, Zimmer **drei-null-fünf**.

@section: 8. Common mistakes

* Forgetting plural **die** even if singular was *das*/*der*.
* Mixing up *vier* and *für* — practice listening.

@section: 9. Lab

1. Write 20 nouns with **articles** (dictionary allowed).
2. Count 1–30 aloud three days in a row and time yourself.
3. Role‑play buying three items and say the total price in German.

@section: 10. Summary

You have **numbers** and the **der/die/das + die(plural)** skeleton. Next: **present tense** and **verb second**.

@section: 11. Time expression preview

*Halb acht* logic differs from English—learn with audio, not only translation.

@section: 12. Gender surprises

*das Mädchen* (girl—neuter due to *‑chen*). Build **image + article** flashcards.

@section: 13. Price dictation

Train *dreiundzwanzig Euro* → digits, then speak back.
