# Greetings, politeness, and the German alphabet

This chapter sets **time‑of‑day greetings**, the **du/Sie** boundary, and the multifunction word ***bitte***. You also learn the **extra letters** ä, ö, ü, ß and how to **spell** your name in German contexts.

@section: 1. Greetings by time

| Phrase | Use |
|--------|-----|
| *Guten Morgen* | Morning |
| *Guten Tag* | Daytime (very common neutral) |
| *Guten Abend* | Evening |
| *Hallo* | Informal |
| *Hi* | Very informal |

Farewells: *Auf Wiedersehen* (formal), *Tschüss* (informal), *Bis bald*.

**Note:** *Gute Nacht* is **goodnight** (farewell/bedtime), not a 9am office hello.

@section: 2. *du* vs *Sie*

* ***Sie*** = formal “you” (also plural polite).
* ***du*** = informal “you”.

In service contexts, use ***Sie*** until invited to *du*.

Examples:

* *Wie heißen **Sie**?*
* *Wie heißt **du**?*

@section: 3. *Bitte* and *Danke*

* *Bitte* can mean **please**, **you’re welcome**, or **here you go** when handing something over.
* *Danke* / *Vielen Dank* = thanks.

Dialogue:

> **A:** Ein Kaffee, **bitte**.  
> **B:** Hier, **bitte**.  
> **A:** **Danke**.  
> **B:** **Bitte**.

@section: 4. Apologies and clarification

* *Entschuldigung*
* *Noch einmal, bitte?*
* *Langsamer, bitte.*

@section: 5. Special letters

* **ä, ö, ü** — umlaut vowels (learn with audio).
* **ß** — *Eszett* (e.g., *Straße*).

Practice spelling:

* *Wie schreibt man das?*

@section: 6. Consonant clusters (A1 level)

* **sch** as /ʃ/ (*Schule*)
* **sp‑/st‑** at syllable onset often /ʃp/, /ʃt/ in standard German (*Sport*, *Stadt*) — with exceptions in compounds; memorize standard patterns first.

@section: 7. First introduction phrases

* *Ich heiße …* / *Mein Name ist …*
* *Ich komme aus …*
* *Ich spreche … und ein bisschen Deutsch.*

Questions:

* *Woher kommen Sie?*
* *Welche Sprachen sprechen Sie?*

@section: 8. Common beginner mistakes

* Skipping formal *Sie* in service settings.
* Thinking *Bitte* only means “please”.
* Mispronouncing **v** (often /f/ in German: *Vater*).

@section: 9. Lab

1. Record 60s: *Guten Tag* + *Ich heiße …* + *Danke* + *Auf Wiedersehen*.
2. Spell an invented German email address aloud.
3. Listen to slow A1 audio and mark every *ch* and *sch* you hear.

@section: 10. Summary

You can greet politely, navigate *du/Sie*, and handle the alphabet with umlauts. Next: **numbers** and **articles**.

@section: 11. Phone and email spelling

Practice: *buchstabieren* your email slowly; distinguish *B* vs *P*, *I* vs *E* for your listener.

@section: 12. Café dialogue (extended)

> **A:** Guten Tag! Möchten Sie etwas trinken?  
> **B:** Einen Tee, **bitte**.  
> **A:** Mit Milch?  
> **B:** Nein, **ohne** Milch, **bitte**.  
> **A:** Hier, **bitte**.  
> **B:** **Danke**.

@section: 13. Shadowing drill

Pick 60s of slow A1 audio; repeat immediately after the speaker focusing on *ch/sch* clusters.
