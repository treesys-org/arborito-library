# 🤝 How to contribute to Arborito Library

Thank you for your interest in growing the forest of knowledge! Arborito is a community project, and every contribution, no matter how small, is valuable.

## 🌟 How Can I Help?

There are many ways to contribute:

*   **Fix Errors:** If you find a typo, a broken link, or a technical inaccuracy, fix it!
*   **Add Content:** Think an important topic is missing? You can propose and write new lessons.
*   **Translate:** Help us bring Arborito to more languages.
*   **Review Content:** If you are an expert in an area, your review is pure gold.

## 🧑‍🏫 We're Looking for Experts!

Arborito's goal is to map a broad spectrum of human knowledge. The current maintainers have a strong background in **Engineering & Technology**, but many other branches of knowledge are started as drafts (often with AI assistance) and need expert review to ensure their quality and accuracy.

If you have professional or academic experience in the following areas, your help is **especially needed**:

*   **🧬 Life & Health Sciences**
*   **⚛️ Exact & Physical Sciences**
*   **🧪 Chemical & Material Sciences**
*   **💼 Business & Leadership**
*   **📜 Humanities & Languages**
*   **⚖️ Law & Political Science**

Reviewing a lesson and giving your approval or suggesting changes is one of the most important contributions you can make.

## 🚀 Contribution Process (GitHub Flow)

We use the standard GitHub Pull Request flow to manage changes.

1.  **Fork the Repository:** Make a copy of the repository in your own GitHub account.
2.  **Create a Branch:** On your fork, create a new branch for your changes (e.g., `fix/typo-in-linux-intro` or `feat/add-quantum-physics-module`).
3.  **Make Your Changes:** Add, edit, or delete files. Be sure to follow the project's style guides.
4.  **Commit Your Changes:** Save your changes with a clear message describing what you did.
5.  **Open a Pull Request (PR):** Submit your changes to the main repository for review. Describe the purpose of your PR and any questions you have.

## 📝 Content Quality Guide

*   **Objectivity:** Content should be neutral and fact-based.
*   **Clarity:** Write simply and directly. Avoid unnecessary jargon.
*   **Atomicity:** Each lesson ("leaf" node) should focus on a single concept.
*   **Sources:** While not mandatory for every lesson, citing reliable sources is highly appreciated, especially on scientific or historical topics.
